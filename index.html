04 · The Journey

Stops along the way

These chapters are not just milestones on a CV — they are training stations where I tested my clarity against the realities of clinical, lab, and community spaces.

Clinical · UCLA Care Extenders

The Hospital Floor

Volunteering in the Neurology department and ER rotations. Escorting families, restocking medical cabinets, and sitting with patients facing critical transitions.

"Between restocking supplies and transporting patients, I found myself sitting at bedsides longer than my tasks required. I learned that the conversations between check-ins — life lessons, gratitude, even shared prayers — carry their own therapeutic weight. These moments confirmed that the physician I want to become is one who treats presence as medicine. I remember standing beside a patient fresh from surgery, still searching for a hand to hold. In those rooms, procedural metrics fade and the human presence becomes the anchor."
Research · CardioNeuroProtection Lab

The Seam of Mind & Body

Wet lab assays (ELISA, Western blots) combined with monthly field research visits to collect biological samples from cancer, PTSD, ALS, and MS patients.

"These retreats were where I needed to be — to collect saliva and cheek scrapes, yes, but also to recalibrate my vision of the future. I remember sitting with one participant before drawing blood, walking her through the consent form. Somewhere in that exchange, the science receded and the person appeared. She was nervous — not about the needle, but about whether any of this would work. I learned my job was not just to collect a sample. It was to make her feel safe enough to believe the study was worth her trust. That is where research and clinical care merge."
Science Communication · Verséa Discovery

Making the Answers Matter

Translating mitochondrial research into accessible communication channels. Managing Dr. Patel's medical profile voluntarily and growing it to 10.7k followers.

"After two years of bench research, I was asked to do something different: communicate the science instead of generating it. I managed Dr. Patel's profile. Once, a mother DMed: her child needed a mitochondrial test, but the only route was a painful muscle biopsy. She asked if our test could yield the same data. I checked with our chief scientist, confirmed it, and the mother was overjoyed. The bench taught me how to ask questions. This role taught me how to make the answers matter to real families."
Leadership · STARS Coordination

Building Lasting Networks

Spent twenty months coordinating research mentorship programs at UC San Diego, serving as a liaison between undergraduates and PhD faculty mentors.

"On paper, the work was logistical — applications, orientations, seminars — but the instinct was the same one I would later bring to patient care. I learned to listen across hierarchies, to translate faculty expectations into student needs, and to build structures that outlasted any single coordinator. True coordination is not administrative; it is human connection made stable."
Community Outreach · Remote Healing

Holding Space at a Distance

Facilitated guided meditation and energy work sessions with 'Healed by the Future' collective, including dedicated 12-week groups for autistic children.

"Facilitating twice-weekly sessions, especially for autistic children, taught me how to lead with presence and unconditional care for individuals I often knew only by name and photograph. I adapted pacing, sensory inputs, and visual triggers. It taught me how to stay grounded in empathy for neurodivergent needs. I learned that presence and patience are clinical interventions in themselves, meeting people where they are."

Academic Publications

Co-Author · Peer Reviewed

Neural and Molecular Changes During a Mind-Body Reconceptualization, Meditation, and Open-Label Placebo Healing Intervention

Published November 5, 2025 · Journal of Mind-Body Regulation. Co-Authored by Fazlalipour, N., Patel, H. et al.

Abstract: This study investigated the physiological and epigenetic changes associated with intensive meditation and open-label placebo interventions. Utilizing metabolic profiling (Seahorse cellular assays), Western blotting, ELISA, and neuroimaging, we mapped metabolic efficiency shifts and functional connectivity in neural networks. The research tracked biological markers in participants living with chronic inflammatory conditions, demonstrating measurable changes in cellular inflammatory output and mitochondrial efficiency post-intervention.
