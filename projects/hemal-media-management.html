2. Content Strategy — Personal Brand, Not Product

The mescreen team handles mitochondria product content. My focus is everything that makes Dr. Patel Dr. Patel — the researcher, the teacher, the scientific voice. These four pillars diversify the account beyond any single product or partnership.

Research Storytelling

186 publications is a content library. Each paper becomes a carousel. Each finding becomes a Reel. Paper-to-post breakdowns — the format that already reached 1.2M views — made recurring. Research is not a one-time announcement. It is the ongoing narrative of the account.

Behind the Lab

A UCSD mitochondrial biology lab is one of the most under-filmed content assets in science communication. Pipetting, cell cultures, the mescreen analyzer, liquid nitrogen — short B-roll Reels with voiceover that make the research tangible. The audience for "what does mitochondrial research actually look like" is massive and underserved.

The Educator

Dr. Patel is a professor before he is anything else. Carousels that teach — "How to read a scientific paper," "What your doctor isn't measuring," "Mitochondria 101." Designed for saves and shares. Content that positions him as the authority without requiring a purchase.

Personal Voice & Philosophy

The account currently informs. It needs to move. Personal reflections on science, mentorship, fatherhood, the research life. The moments between the papers. This is the pillar that builds emotional connection — the reason someone follows him and not just the research.

3. Instagram Broadcast Channel

A direct line to the audience — no algorithm, no feed ranking

Instagram's broadcast channel feature gives @hemalpatelphd a dedicated space to share longer-form insights without competing for feed placement. Subscribers opt in and receive notifications — every message lands. This becomes the distribution engine for:

  • Blog links — full essay versions of the ideas teased in Reels and carousels
  • Scientific concepts — deeper dives that are too long for a caption, too nuanced for a 60-second Reel
  • Paper summaries — "What I'm reading this week" — a curation of published work with brief context from Dr. Patel
  • Personal notes — conference takeaways, lab updates, reflections that don't need a full post
  • Early access — subscribers hear about new content, collaborations, and appearances before the feed does

The channel is a lightweight, high-impact addition. One hour per week of my time keeps it active — and it gives Dr. Patel an owned audience channel that no algorithm change can touch.
