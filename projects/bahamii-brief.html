1. The best ingredient in this brand isn't in the bar

01

You have a founder story that most CPG brands at 100K followers would trade their agency retainer for

Three brothers. Persian great-grandmother Parvin who loved dates but couldn't handle sugar. Your mother's kitchen. Two blenders destroyed — one nearly caused a house fire. The name Bahamii comes from Baham, the Farsi word for "together." An 18-month sprint from home recipe to 25 Costco locations.

Right now: Your Instagram bio reads like a nutrition label — "Date & Almond Bars. Fiber. Gluten Free. No Added Sugar." Your feed is product-forward. The story that makes Bahamii different from every other clean snack bar on the shelf is barely visible. Your founder, Noah Nazarian, has an Instagram account (@noah.nazarian) but doesn't appear on the brand feed. You're selling a bar when you should be selling a family.

💡 This is fixable in one filming session. Point a camera at Noah for 90 minutes. Four 60-second Reels: (1) the great-grandmother, (2) the smoking blender, (3) the Costco call, (4) what Bahamii means. Post one per week for a month. Founder-led content is the #1 trust builder for emerging CPG brands. You have a better story than anyone on your shelf. You're just not telling it.

2. You're in 25 Costco locations and your Instagram doesn't show it

02

The most impressive thing about Bahamii at 4,500 followers is that it's on Costco shelves

Most food brands at 4.5K followers are still in farmer's markets. You have a regional Costco rollout, Amazon nationwide, and a 5-star review profile. The brand is ahead of its Instagram — not the other way around. But the feed treats this like a footnote.

The gap: No Costco endcap Reel. No "here's our variety pack on the shelf" walk-up video. No sampling station content. No "I found Bahamii at Costco" UGC reshare pipeline. The Costco box promotes your Youth Venture Program with Boys & Girls Clubs — and the Instagram doesn't mention it. You have 5,000 students reached through a program that teaches entrepreneurship and healthy eating. That's a content pillar, not a packaging footnote.

💡 Every Costco shopper who follows you from the aisle is a retained customer with 2–3× the lifetime value. The bridge between "I saw this at Costco" and "I follow this brand" is a Reel of your endcap and a Story sticker that says "Found us? Tag us." Right now that bridge doesn't exist.

4. The opportunity — and what it would take

→

4.5K → 15K followers in 90 days, 4%+ engagement, retail-to-social flywheel active

This isn't a growth-hack projection. It's the result of five structural shifts that take about 90 minutes of filming per week and one consistent posting calendar:

1. Founder story arc. Four Reels across one month. Noah on camera. The origin, the mission, the Costco journey. This is the trust engine.
2. Retail-to-social bridge. One Reel/week showing the Costco endcap, the sampling station, the variety pack on the shelf. Every Costco shopper who follows from the aisle is a retained customer.
3. UGC pipeline. "Found us at Costco? Tag @eatbahamii." Repost the best one each week. One campaign = infinite content.
4. Cultural storytelling. "Why dates are the superfood of Persian culture." "What Bahamii means." One carousel/week. This is your IP. No competitor has it.
5. Impact content. Feature one student from the Youth Venture Program. Let them tell their story. The program reached 5,000 students. The feed should show at least one.
