🟢 What's Working

Strength 1

A founder story most CPG brands would kill for

Three brothers. Persian great-grandmother. Home kitchen R&D. Two destroyed blenders. Farsi wordplay in the brand name. 18 months from a smoking blender to a Costco endcap. This is a documentary — and it's not on the feed.

Strength 2

Retail credibility at a 4,500-follower scale

25 Costco locations + Amazon nationwide is real. Most brands at Bahamii's follower count are still DTC-only. The "as seen at Costco" signal is a trust shortcut no competitor at this tier can claim. It's in the bio. It's not in the content.

🔴 What's Missing

Gap 1

The shelf-to-social bridge doesn't exist

No Costco aisle Reel. No sampling station content. No endcap walk-up. No "here's what our shelf looks like." The most impressive thing about Bahamii — the retail presence — is treated like a footnote on the feed. Shoppers who find the bar in-store have nothing to follow back to.

Gap 2

5,000 students reached — zero on the feed

The Youth Venture Program with Boys & Girls Club is printed on the Costco box. It's the brand's social impact IP. Impact content outperforms product content by 2–3× for CPG brands at this tier. The program exists. The content doesn't. Every student story is a post waiting to be made.

🔍 Diagnosis: Bahamii has more brand substance than any peer at 4.5K followers — and posts less of it than all of them. The story, the shelf, and the social impact are real. The feed just shows the bar.

⚡ Three Moves

1

Film the founder story arc — 4 Reels, one filming session

Noah Nazarian on camera. 60 seconds each. Chapter 1: the Persian great-grandmother. Chapter 2: the smoking blender and the home kitchen. Chapter 3: the Costco call. Chapter 4: what "Bahamii" means. Post one per week. This is the highest-converting content Bahamii owns — and it hasn't been made yet.

2

Build the retail-to-social bridge — 2 Reels this weekend

Walk into a SoCal Costco. Film the endcap. Film the variety pack on the shelf. End with: "Find us at 25 Costco locations. Link in bio." Then launch a "Tag Your Bahamii" UGC campaign — shoppers who find the bar in-store and post it get reposted + entered into a monthly giveaway. One campaign = infinite content.

3

Rewrite the bio + post one Youth Venture Reel

Current bio is a nutrition label. New bio: "Three brothers. One Persian great-grandmother. The date bar that went from a home kitchen to Costco in 18 months." Then feature one student from the Boys & Girls Club program. Let them tell their story. 5,000 students reached — the feed should show at least one of them.
