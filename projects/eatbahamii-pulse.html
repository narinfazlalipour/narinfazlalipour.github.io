1. The Product — What's in the Bar

Bahamii makes date and almond bars in three flavors: Chocolate Almond, Coconut Vanilla, and Honey Cinnamon. Dates are the #1 ingredient — no added sugar, no preservatives, no seed oils, no palm oil. Gluten-free, dairy-free, soy-free, Non-GMO Project Verified, Kosher certified. The bars are soft, chewy, and positioned as a clean-energy snack for the Costco crowd and the Amazon cart alike.

The product is legitimately clean. The ingredient list reads like a pantry, not a lab. In a category where "clean label" is often marketing, Bahamii's formulation is the real thing — dates as binder and sweetener, almonds for protein and texture, superfoods for differentiation. The bars launched from a home kitchen to 25 Costco locations in Southern California in 18 months. Most CPG startups don't make that shelf. Bahamii did.

💡 Product truth: The bar is good. The Costco placement is real. The clean-label claims are defensible. The Instagram just hasn't caught up to the shelf presence.

2. The Story — The Best Ingredient They're Not Posting

Bahamii's founding story is better than most CPG brands at any tier. Three brothers (Noah, Aaron, Jonah) were inspired by their great-grandmother Parvin ("Mamane"), who loved dates — a Persian superfood staple — but needed a sweet treat that wouldn't spike her blood sugar. The recipe was developed in their mother's kitchen. Noah burned through two blenders, one of which smoked and nearly caused a house fire. The name "Bahamii" comes from the Farsi word Baham — "together" — a double meaning that ties dates (the ingredient) to dates (the occasion).

This is a first-generation immigrant family startup story with a Costco ending. The brand also runs the Bahamii Youth Venture Program, which has reached over 5,000 students through a partnership with Boys & Girls Clubs, teaching financial literacy, entrepreneurship, and healthy eating. The social impact isn't a footnote — it's printed on the Costco box.

The Instagram bio captures none of this. It says: "Date & Almond Bars @costco @amazon. Fiber. Gluten & Dairy Free. No Preservatives. No Added Sugar." That's a product description, not a brand story. The gap between the founding narrative and the feed narrative is the single biggest content opportunity.

4. What's Working — Brand Assets on the Feed

Asset 1

Genuine Retail Credibility

Costco placement in 25 locations and Amazon availability are real. Most 4.5K-follower food brands don't have big-box distribution. The "as seen at Costco" signal is a trust shortcut that most DTC snack brands can't claim. It's in the bio — but the feed doesn't leverage the brag.

Asset 2

A Founders' Story Worth Telling

Three brothers. Persian great-grandmother. Home kitchen R&D. Two destroyed blenders. Farsi wordplay in the brand name. First-gen immigrant entrepreneurship. This is a documentary, not a brand deck. Founder Noah Nazarian (@noah.nazarian) is the face of the brand — but the Instagram feed hasn't put him front and center.

Asset 3

Clean Visual Identity

The Bahamii brand identity — warm neutrals, date-toned palette, clean typography — is consistent and premium-feeling. Product photography is solid. The packaging reads as modern CPG, not homemade startup. In the snack bar category, visual polish signals product quality before the first bite.

Asset 4

Built-in Social Impact

The Youth Venture Program with Boys & Girls Clubs has reached 5,000+ students. This isn't a CSR checkbox — it's a genuine community program that teaches entrepreneurship. It's on the Costco box. It should be a content pillar on the feed. Impact content outperforms product content by 2–3× on engagement for CPG brands at this tier.

5. What's Missing — Content White Space

Gap 1

The Founder Story Is in Bio, Not in Feed

The bio is a product spec sheet. The origin — three brothers, Persian great-grandmother, smoking blender, 18 months to Costco — is the highest-converting content the brand owns. It's not on the feed. Founder-led content is the #1 trust builder for emerging CPG brands. It's absent.

Gap 2

Zero Customer UGC or Testimonials

Costco shoppers trying the bars. Amazon reviews. "I found these at Costco and they're my new 3pm snack." This is free content that converts — and it's nowhere on the feed. The brand has 5-star reviews across platforms and zero UGC Reels. Every review is a post waiting to be made.

Gap 3

No Behind-the-Scenes Production Content

How are date bars made? What does the facility look like? Who's on the team? CPG brands that show production BTS build trust that product-only feeds can't. The "how it's made" format is a proven engagement driver for food brands — and Bahamii has zero of it.

Gap 4

Retail-to-Social Bridge Is Missing

"Find us at Costco" is in the bio. But there's no Costco aisle Reel, no "trying Bahamii at the Costco sampling station," no "here's what our endcap looks like." The retail presence is the most impressive thing about the brand at this stage — and the feed treats it like a footnote.

Gap 5

No Cultural Storytelling

Bahamii = Baham = "together" in Farsi. Dates are a Persian superfood staple. The great-grandmother story. The immigrant family entrepreneurship arc. This is cultural IP that differentiates the brand from every other clean snack bar on the shelf. It's referenced in the brand name and nowhere in the content strategy.

Gap 6

Minimal Reels Strategy

380 posts suggests active posting — but the format mix leans product-photo-heavy. Reels are the format that reaches non-followers on Instagram's current algorithm. CPG brands at this tier that run 3+ Reels/week grow 2–3× faster than photo-only accounts. The category is visual. The format should match.

7. Growth Levers — What Moves the Needle

🎯 Target: 4.5K → 15K Followers, Retail-to-Social Bridge Active

The core unlock: Bahamii is a retail brand that happens to have an Instagram. It should be a story brand that happens to sell at Costco. Every post should make someone want to find the bar — in the aisle, on Amazon, or through the link in bio.

1

Founder Face-to-Camera — 2×/week

Noah Nazarian on camera for 60 seconds. The story of the three brothers, the smoking blender, the Persian great-grandmother, the 18-month sprint to Costco. This is the single highest-leverage format for an emerging CPG brand. Three modes to rotate:

"The Origin" (monthly series): Chapter 1: "Our great-grandmother loved dates but couldn't handle sugar." Chapter 2: "My mom's kitchen. Two destroyed blenders. One near house fire." Chapter 3: "The call from Costco." Chapter 4: "What 'Bahamii' actually means."
"Behind the Brand" (weekly): Noah in the facility. Noah at Costco. Noah reading a 5-star review. Noah explaining why dates are the #1 ingredient.
"The Mission" (weekly): Youth Venture Program updates. Students building businesses. The impact beyond the bar.

2

Retail-to-Social Bridge Reels — 2×/week

The Costco placement is the most impressive thing about Bahamii at this stage. Make it content:
• "Find us at Costco" — aisle walk-up Reel showing the endcap
• Sampling station footage — real shoppers trying the bars for the first time
• "What $18.65 gets you at Costco" — variety pack unboxing
• Side-by-side: "The Costco shelf vs. our home kitchen 18 months ago"
Every Costco shopper who follows the brand from the store to Instagram is a retained customer with higher LTV.

3

Customer UGC Pipeline — 1×/week

The brand has 5-star reviews on Amazon and Costco.com. Turn them into content:
• "Review of the Week" — screenshot of a real review with voiceover
• "You tried Bahamii at Costco — tag us" campaign with a monthly giveaway
• DM request template: "Love that you grabbed Bahamii! Can we share your photo?"
One customer Reel = 50 product posts in trust-building power.

4

Cultural Storytelling Carousels — 1×/week

Bahamii = Baham = "together" in Farsi. This is the brand's IP. Carousel ideas:
• "Why dates are the superfood of Persian culture"
• "What 'Bahamii' means — the double meaning explained"
• "The three brothers behind the bar" — one slide per brother
• "From Tehran to Costco — our family's food story"
Cultural storytelling is the moat. No competitor has it. Bahamii is sitting on it.

5

How It's Made — 1×/week

CPG brands that show production build trust that product-only feeds can't. Format:
• 30–60 second BTS Reels: dates being processed, bars being packaged, the facility line
• "One bar, eight ingredients" — each ingredient gets a close-up
• "This is what 'no preservatives' actually looks like"
The "how it's made" format has a proven engagement pattern for food brands — transparency = trust.

8. This Week — Five Actions

Film Noah telling the origin story in 4 parts

One filming session. Four 60-second Reels: (1) the great-grandmother, (2) the smoking blender, (3) the Costco call, (4) what Bahamii means. Post one per week for a month. This is the founder story arc — the highest-retention content format on the platform for CPG brands.

Film a Costco aisle walk-up Reel this weekend

Walk into a Southern California Costco. Film the endcap. Show the variety pack on the shelf. End with: "Find us in 25 Costco locations across SoCal. Link in bio for Amazon." This is the bridge between the shelf and the social account.

Launch a "Tag Your Bahamii" UGC campaign

Story sticker: "Found us at Costco? Tag @eatbahamii." Repost the best one each week. Monthly giveaway: free variety pack for the best submission. One campaign = infinite content.

Rewrite the bio to tell the story

Current: "Date & Almond Bars @costco @amazon. Fiber. Gluten & Dairy Free." → New: "Three brothers. One Persian great-grandmother. The date bar that went from a home kitchen to Costco in 18 months. 100% clean. Zero added sugar. @costco @amazon." The bio should make someone want to follow — not just scan a nutrition label.

Post one Youth Venture Program Reel

Feature a student from the Boys & Girls Club program. Let them tell their story. Tag @bgcwgsv. Impact content outperforms product content by 2–3× on engagement. The program has reached 5,000 students. The feed should show at least one of them.
