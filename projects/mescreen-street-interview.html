The Approach

Conversational, person-on-the-street. No clipboard energy. I act as both interviewer and someone who genuinely knows the science — because I do. Questions are designed to open people up, not box them in, and mescreen™ is introduced naturally in conversation rather than as a hard pitch. The science sells itself when explained by someone who understands it.

  1. "When you hear the word mitochondria, what comes to mind?"
  2. "Be honest — have you thought about your cells today?"
  3. "When you feel tired, where do you think that tiredness lives?"
  4. "What do you do to boost your energy?"
  5. "Do you ever think about your energy at a cellular level?"
  6. "If you could measure your body's energy production — like a fuel gauge — would you want to know?"
  7. "There's now an at-home test — a few drops of blood — that tells you how well your mitochondria are working. What's your reaction to that?"
  8. "It was originally developed for NASA's Twin Study. Does knowing the science pedigree change how you feel about it?"
  9. "The test is $699. Given what you now know about mitochondria, does that feel worth it to you?"
  10. "What would it change for your life to know your cellular energy score?"
