What's Missing From the Feed

GAP 01

The Founder Story Is Nowhere

Noah Nazarian's journey — great-grandmother's love of dates, the Farsi meaning of Bahamii, burning through two blenders in his mom's kitchen — is the kind of story that builds emotional connection with buyers. Right now, the feed doesn't tell it at all. A Costco shopper sees a date bar; they don't see the first-gen founder who refused to compromise on ingredients.

What's there now: UGC reposts and Costco location announcements. No founder content, no origin storytelling.
GAP 02

The Social Impact Is Invisible

Bahamii's Youth Venture Program has taught 5,000+ students financial literacy and entrepreneurship through the Boys & Girls Club. That's rare. Most CPG brands bolt on a charity partnership; Bahamii built a self-sustaining curriculum. This belongs on the feed — photos from sessions, student stories, curriculum highlights. It's a trust-builder, a differentiator, and exactly the kind of content that earns saves and shares.

What's there now: No mention of the Youth Venture Program on Instagram. The packaging carries it — the feed doesn't.
GAP 03

The Feed Is Almost Entirely UGC Reposts

Scrolling @eatbahamii feels like reading a Costco influencer campaign dashboard — #izzybahamii, #costcofinds, #ad. UGC has its place, but when it's 90%+ of the grid, the brand has no voice of its own. There's no product education, no usage inspiration, no behind-the-scenes, no community. The brand is outsourcing its entire visual identity to micro-influencers, and the result is a feed that looks like everyone else's Costco snack page.

What's there now: Nearly every visible post is an Izzy Group influencer reel with #izzybahamii. Organic brand content is buried or non-existent.
GAP 04

No Visual Identity or Grid Rhythm

The feed lacks a recognizable visual system — no consistent color palette, no typography hierarchy, no grid pattern. When someone lands on the profile, they should know within 3 seconds that this is Bahamii. Right now, it looks like a generic snack repost page. The warm date-browns, the gold from honey, the cream from coconut — these are beautiful colors that aren't being used intentionally.

What's there now: Inconsistent thumbnails, no brand color system, no visual through-line across posts.
GAP 05

No Community or Conversation

4,558 followers after 382 posts, a Costco launch, and paid influencer campaigns is a signal. The content drives awareness but not connection. There's no reply strategy visible, no community-building content, no reason for someone to come back to the profile after they've bought a box. The feed broadcasts; it doesn't build.

What's there now: One-directional posting. No engagement-forward content (polls, Q&As, community spotlights, user recipe features).
Diagnosis

Bahamii has distribution without differentiation on Instagram. The product is in Costco — that's the hard part. But the feed is doing what every other CPG brand does: repost UGC and announce retail wins. The brand story, the founder journey, the social impact, the ingredient philosophy — the things that make Bahamii Bahamii — are absent. The feed is functional. It could be magnetic.

Where I Fit

Media management for Bahamii — strategy, content, and community in one seat

🧠

Content Strategy & Creative Direction

  • Build the content pillars that are missing — founder story, social impact, product education, community
  • Develop a visual identity system: color palette, typography, grid rhythm, thumbnail templates
  • Plan the weekly content calendar across Reels, carousels, and Stories
  • Write captions that tell stories, not just announce retail wins
🎬

Content Creation

  • Original Reels — product storytelling, behind-the-scenes, founder features, impact highlights
  • Designed carousels for ingredient education, brand values, press features
  • Story content: daily presence, polls, Q&As, community spotlights
  • UGC curation — keep the best influencer content, but make it 20% of the feed, not 90%
💬

Community Management

  • Daily engagement — reply to comments, DMs, and tagged posts with a real voice
  • Build conversation-forward content: ask questions, run polls, feature customer stories
  • Nurture the Costco-to-Instagram pipeline — shoppers who discover in-warehouse should find a feed worth following
📊

Performance & Reporting

  • Track what's working — engagement rate, Reels views, follower growth, save rate
  • Monthly performance reports with insights and adjustments
  • Competitive monitoring — what are other better-for-you snack brands doing on IG?
  • Quarterly brand pulse audits to track progress against the strategy

The Open Lane

Bahamii already won the hard battle — getting on shelves. The product is in Costco, on Amazon, in Kroger. People are trying it. They're posting about it. But the Instagram feed isn't converting that discovery into loyalty. It's a retail announcement board when it could be a brand home — a place where people come to learn the story, see the impact, feel the care behind the ingredients, and become the kind of customer who buys boxes, not bars. The feed just needs to catch up to the brand.
